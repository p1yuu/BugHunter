The CIS Critical Security Controls (CIS Controls) define a prioritized set of actions that organizations can take to reduce the most common and impactful cybersecurity risks.



Unlike vulnerability lists, CIS Controls focus on practical, defensive measures that have proven effective in real-world environments.



\## Purpose of CIS Controls

\- Provide a clear security baseline

\- Help organizations focus on what matters most

\- Reduce attack surface through consistent control implementation



\## Control Philosophy

CIS Controls emphasize:

\- Defense-in-depth

\- Measurable and repeatable security practices

\- Alignment between technical controls and operational processes



\## Examples of Control Domains (High-Level)

Key areas covered by CIS Controls include:

\- Asset inventory and visibility

\- Secure configuration of systems

\- Continuous vulnerability management

\- Controlled use of administrative privileges

\- Audit logging and monitoring

\- Incident response preparedness



\## Risk Perspective

When security gaps align with missing or weak CIS Controls, it often indicates:

\- Immature security operations

\- Lack of standardized security baselines

\- Increased likelihood of successful attacks



\## Assessment Implications

From a research and assessment perspective, CIS Controls help identify:

\- Which control gaps may warrant deeper review

\- Whether risks are isolated or systemic

\- Opportunities for meaningful risk reduction through process improvement



CIS Controls are especially useful for translating technical risk into actionable security programs.
